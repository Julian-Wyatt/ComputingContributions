Artifical Intelligence

What does “Artificial Intelligence” even mean?

Generally, the term “artificial intellgence” is used when a machine can achieve tasks usually associated with human-like cognitive abilities, such as learning and problem solving.

Within artificial intelligence there are many different areas, most notably:

  • Knowledge reasoning
  • Planning
  • Machine learning
  • Natural language processing
  • Computer vision
  • Robotics
  • Artificial general intelligence

How does this help society?

Previously many tasks had to be done slowly by hand, often taking lots of hours of work. With these new techniques many different processes can be automated, such as fraud detection within banking, airport criminal detection and plagairism checking. Not only can they be automated, they can be much faster and more accurate than a human counterpart. Below are some examples of areas that artificial intelligence is particularly good at.

Social Networking

Most social networking applications use artificial intelligence. For instance, Facebook automatically highlights faces and suggests friends to tag, personalizes your news feed to ensure you're seeing posts that interest you and target you with adverts that you're more likely to click on. In June 2016, Facebook announced “DeepText”. A text understanding engine that, the company claims “can understand with near-human accuracy the textual content of several thousand posts per second, spanning more than 20 languages.” DeepText is used in Facebook Messenger to detect intent. For example, by realising you want to hail an Uber from within the app when you message “I need a ride”.
